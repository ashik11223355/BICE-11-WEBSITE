Frequently Asked Questions

How often can I donate blood? +
You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donors can donate every 7 days, up to 24 times per year. This allows your body time to replenish the donated blood components.
Is blood donation safe? +
Yes, blood donation is completely safe. We use sterile, single-use needles and equipment for each donor. There is no risk of contracting any disease from donating blood.
What are the eligibility requirements? +
You must be at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some states), weigh at least 110 pounds, and be in good general health. Additional requirements may apply based on travel history and medications.
How long does the donation process take? +
The entire process takes about 45-60 minutes. The actual blood collection takes only 8-12 minutes. The rest of the time is spent on registration, health screening, and recovery.
What should I do before and after donating? +
Before: Eat a healthy meal, drink plenty of water, and get a good night's sleep. After: Continue drinking fluids, avoid heavy lifting for 24 hours, and eat iron-rich foods to help replenish your blood.
